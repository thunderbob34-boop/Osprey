Screen annotations

1
Recovery Score (hero). Circular progress ring, 0–100. Green = good (70+), amber = caution (40–69), red = poor (<40). Three sub-metrics below: HRV, sleep, resting HR with delta indicators.
2
Today's Session card. Session type badge, name, target metrics, and a single Start Session CTA. If Ozzie rescheduled today, shows the new session with a Why? link. Never shows a guilt-inducing "missed" state.
3
Nutrition strip. Calories consumed vs. target, plus three macro progress bars. Tapping anywhere opens the food log. On rest days, targets auto-adjust down — Ozzie explains why.
4
Ozzie's insight card. Always at the bottom of the summary. One specific, data-backed insight in plain English. The "Why?" button opens a bottom sheet showing the exact data points behind any plan change — always stored at the time of the decision, never regenerated.
5
Why? bottom sheet. Shows the 3–4 data points that drove the decision, with Ozzie's plain-language explanation. Dismissed with a single tap. This is the trust engine — users who understand Ozzie's decisions become long-term users.

Onboarding flow notes

1
Ozzie intro (screen 1). Ozzie leads with his name and personality, not a feature list. One speech bubble sets the tone for the whole relationship. "I'm not going to throw a complicated setup at you" is the first thing he says — trust starts here.
2
5 form screens, max. Goal → Fitness level → Target event → Schedule → Plan reveal. No account setup friction here — email/password is handled before this flow. Progress bar visible throughout.
3
Option cards, not dropdowns. Every question uses large tap targets with icons and one-line descriptions. No typing unless entering a race date or goal time. Fast, thumb-friendly, zero friction.
4
Ozzie closes with the plan reveal. He references the user's specific answers ("4 days a week, race Oct 12th, goal 4:15") to prove he was actually listening. The weekly tile view makes the plan tangible immediately.
5
"Adjust the plan" always visible. The plan is a starting point, not a verdict. Giving users control from the first screen builds trust and reduces churn from feeling locked in.
6
Wearable connect deferred. Ozzie prompts wearable connection on day 2, not during onboarding. One action at a time. Users who haven't connected a wearable still get a useful plan — missing data is never a blocker.

Active Workout — Running GPS

1
Floating HUD tiles overlay the map — glass cards with time, distance, and current pace. Pace tile color-codes to zone: green = tempo, amber = threshold, red = too fast.
2
Live map shows planned route (dashed) vs completed route (solid teal), current position dot with pace zone glow ring, and numbered mile markers.
3
Pace Zone bar is a continuous gradient from Easy → Tempo → Threshold → Max with a needle showing real-time effort. Whole card turns amber when outside target zone.
4
Ozzie live cue appears mid-run with mile-by-mile context. On the "Too Fast" screen, Ozzie fires a negative split warning before the effort becomes a problem.
5
Split comparison table shows every completed mile vs target, going amber/red when drift exceeds ±10 seconds/mile — negative split intelligence in plain sight.
6
Pause screen collapses the map, shows a summary stat row, and Ozzie gives a mid-workout status briefing. Resume or end from here.
