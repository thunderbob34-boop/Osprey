# PROJECT OSPREY — The Pool Racing Blueprint
*Your Expert Coach system for training, technique, and nutrition — sprint to distance*

This is the swimming edition of the OSPREY Expert Coach program — the same engine that powers the cycling and ultra plans, rebuilt lane-by-lane for the pool. It turns four onboarding answers into a personalized, periodized plan that makes you faster without wrecking your shoulders. Written for the athlete: plain language, real numbers, zero fluff.

## 1. How the Expert Coach Program Works

Every OSPREY plan is built from four onboarding inputs. Answer honestly and the engine maps you onto the right training volume, intensity mix, and race approach.

| # | Onboarding Input | What it drives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Experience & current load — years swimming, yards/week, sessions/week | Starting volume, progression rate, technique vs. fitness emphasis |
| 2 | Goal event(s) & primary stroke — 50 to 1500, plus IM | Energy-system mix, race-pace sets, taper length |
| 3 | Timeline to peak meet — weeks until target | Macrocycle structure (Base/Build/Peak weeks) |
| 4 | Constraints & injury history — schedule, pool access, shoulders/back | Weekly session count, dryland design, load caps and red flags |

**One-line philosophy:** Build a big aerobic engine, sharpen it with race-specific speed, protect the shoulders, and arrive at the meet rested and sharp.

## 2. Training Philosophy

**Intensity distribution — pyramidal base, polarized sharpening.** Early season is pyramidal (lots of easy aerobic, a solid threshold block, a little speed). As the target meet nears it polarizes: most work stays easy, the hard work gets genuinely hard and race-specific, and the grey middle shrinks.

| Macrocycle | Weeks out | Emphasis | Looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | Furthest | Aerobic capacity + technique | Higher volume, CSS/threshold, drill-heavy |
| Build | Mid-season | Threshold → VO2 max | Race-pace intervals appear, kick/pull power, dryland peaks |
| Peak | Weeks before | VO2 + race pace | Lower volume, sharp descending sets, broken race swims |
| Taper | Final 1–3 wks | Freshness + feel | Volume cut 40–60%, intensity kept, full recovery |

**Critical Swim Speed (CSS)** is your functional threshold pace and training anchor. Field test: all-out 400, full recovery, all-out 200.
- **CSS per 100 = (400 time in sec − 200 time in sec) ÷ 2.**
- Example: 400 in 6:00 (360s), 200 in 2:52 (172s) → (360−172)÷2 = 94s = **1:34 / 100**.
- Re-test every 4–6 weeks. CSS ≈ 90–95% of 400 pace, ≈ 80–85% of best 100 speed.

**Race-pace training (USRPT, used sparingly).** Swim at exactly goal race pace in short reps with short rest, stopping before form falls apart. Targeted tool in Build/Peak — not the whole program (a pure race-pace diet under-builds the aerobic base and over-stresses shoulders).

**The five zones** (paces set as offsets from CSS):

| Zone | Name | Effort vs CSS | Purpose | Rest |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Z1 | Easy/Recovery | CSS +8s or slower | Base, recovery, technique | Continuous/short |
| Z2 | Aerobic | CSS +3–6s /100 | Build engine, economy | 10–20s |
| Z3 | Threshold (CSS) | CSS ±2s /100 | Lactate clearance | 10–15s |
| Z4 | VO2 max | CSS −2 to −5s /100 | Max oxygen uptake | 1:1 work:rest |
| Z5 | Race/Sprint | Goal pace / all-out | Speed, power, specificity | Full (2–4× work) |

## 3. The Key Sessions

- **Aerobic/threshold (bread and butter):** CSS intervals (10–20×100 at CSS on 10–15s rest); descending sets (200s Z2→Z4); long aerobic 800–1500 at Z2.
- **VO2/speed-endurance:** short reps at 1:1 (8–12×50 fast on :60, 6×100 at 400 effort); broken swims (race distance split with short rest).
- **Race-pace (USRPT-style):** 100 goal → 16×25 at goal-100 pace; 200 goal → 20×50; 400/500 → 30×50 or 15×100. Stop when you miss twice.
- **Sprint/lactate (50–100 racers):** all-out 15–25s with full recovery; dive 25s, push 50s, underwater speed. Low volume, high quality.
- **Technique, kick, pull:** drills every session (sculling, catch-up, fingertip drag, single-arm, 6-kick switch); dedicated kick sets; pull sets with buoy ± paddles (cap paddle volume early).

## 4. Technique & Stroke Mechanics

Races are won on economy as much as fitness. Technique is permanent, not a beginner phase.
1. **Body line & balance** — swim "downhill," hips high, head neutral. Drag is the enemy.
2. **The catch** — early vertical forearm; grab water with the whole forearm.
3. **Distance per stroke vs. stroke rate** — know your optimal count per event.
4. **Breathing** — bilateral in training; controlled, low breath.
5. **Walls & underwaters** — starts, turns, first 15m off each wall win races.

**Shoulder-first swimming:** high-elbow catch (no straight-arm windmill), no thumb-first entry, controlled paddle progression, dryland balancing pulls with rear-shoulder/rotator-cuff work. Shoulder pain is a STOP signal.

## 5. Dryland & Strength

Periodized with the pool: general strength in Base, power in Build, maintenance/sharpness in Peak/Taper.

| Phase | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Movement quality + general strength | Pull-ups, rows, squats, hinges, core, band prehab |
| Build | Max strength → power | Trap-bar deadlift, weighted pulls, med-ball throws |
| Peak | Power / rate of force | Explosive low-rep lifts, jumps, start & streamline |
| Taper | Maintenance + activation | Light, snappy, low-volume; add no fatigue |

**Non-negotiable weekly prehab:** rotator-cuff external rotation, scapular retraction/rows, thoracic mobility, posterior chain.

## 6. Nutrition & Hydration

**Daily carbohydrate:**

| Day type | Carb target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Easy / technique / low volume | 3–5 g/kg/day | Keep protein and veg high |
| Moderate volume + intensity | 5–7 g/kg/day | Everyday range |
| High volume / doubles | 8–10 g/kg/day | Big aerobic days |
| Very high / peak week | 10–12 g/kg/day | Heaviest blocks only |

**Protein:** ~1.5–2.0 g/kg/day, ~0.3 g/kg per meal, a dose within 30–60 min of hard sessions.

**Around training:** pre-session (3–4h) carb-forward meal ~1–2 g/kg, low fat/fibre; top-up 30–60 min before if needed; recovery within 30–60 min = carbs + 20–40 g protein (crucial before a 2nd session).

**Hydration:** ~3–5 L/day baseline (more in heat); 2% body-mass loss slows you; electrolyte drink at 4–8% carb with sodium on long/hot sessions. **Don't over-drink** — beyond thirst risks hyponatremia (<135 mmol/L).

**Meet day:** graze 25–60 g carbs/hour between swims (up to ~90 g/hr for long demanding sessions); sip electrolyte on deck; nothing new on race day.

## 7. Race Preparation by Event

| Event | Dominant energy system | Training emphasis | Race strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50 | Alactic/anaerobic power | Speed, starts, underwaters, dryland power | All-out; win start & breakout; minimal breathing |
| 100 | Anaerobic + high aerobic | Speed-endurance, race-pace 25s, lactate tolerance | Fast controlled first 50, hold form on the back half |
| 200 | Aerobic-anaerobic blend | Race-pace 50s, threshold, pace discipline | Even/slightly negative split; strong 3rd 50 |
| 400/500 | Strongly aerobic | CSS/threshold volume, race-pace 100s, kick | Settle rhythm, negative split, build last 100–150 |
| 800/1500/1650 | Aerobic engine | High aerobic volume, CSS sets, efficiency | Metronome pacing, relaxed stroke, finish hard |
| 200/400 IM | All systems + skill | All four strokes, transitions, race-pace by stroke | Don't overcook fly; hold back/breast; win the free |

## 8. Taper & Race Day

The taper is a precise reduction, not rest: cut volume **40–60% over the final 1–3 weeks while KEEPING intensity and race-pace touches** → swimmers improve ~2–3%. Volume-down-only tapers leave that gain on the table.

| Lever | Do | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Volume | Reduce 40–60% (sprinters further) | Sheds fatigue, restores force |
| Intensity | Keep it — short race-pace touches | Maintains feel and sharpness |
| Frequency | Keep most sessions, shorter | Preserves rhythm/technique |
| Duration | Sprinters ~1–2 wks; distance ~2–3 wks | Longer events shed more fatigue |

**Race day:** warm up fully (easy swim, build 50s, race-pace efforts, starts/turns, stay warm); fuel light and familiar 2–3h before; own your splits/stroke count/breakouts; swim-down between prelims and finals, refuel, re-warm.

## 9. Staying Healthy — Load & Red Flags

The .01% difference is consecutive healthy weeks. Progress ≤~10% volume/week, bank recovery. STOP signals:
- Shoulder pain during/after swimming (painful arc on pull-through / overhead) — back off, cut paddle/pull, ramp prehab, assess if it lingers.
- Sharp/worsening low-back or (breaststroke) knee pain — modify stroke and volume.
- Persistent fatigue, elevated resting HR, poor sleep, stalling times — under-recovery; take the recovery weeks.
- Any pain that alters your stroke — fix the cause; compensating spreads injury.

**Load rules:** build gradually then recovery week every 3–4 wks; add hard intensity OR big volume, rarely both; cap early-season paddle/pull; aerobic base is safe to build, top-end speed is where injuries hide.

## 10. Four Swimmer Profiles

- **The Returning Adult** (3 swims/wk, goal 200 free): rebuild technique + aerobic base first; conservative volume +10%/wk, recovery week every 4th; heavy drills; CSS test at wk 4.
- **The Age-Group Sprinter** (teen, 5–6 swims/wk, 50/100): speed, starts, underwaters, lactate tolerance; race-pace 25s/50s; explosive dryland; 1–2 wk taper; aerobic base maintained.
- **The Masters Distance Swimmer** (40s, 4 swims/wk, 1500/open water): CSS/threshold volume, stroke economy; metronomic pacing, kick capacity; shoulder prehab non-negotiable.
- **The IM All-Rounder** (college, 8+ swims/wk, 200/400 IM): all four strokes, transitions, race-pace by leg; full periodization; peaks with broken IMs; power + rigorous prehab.
