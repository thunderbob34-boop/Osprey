# PROJECT OSPREY — The Road-Racing Blueprints
*Your Expert Coach system for training, fueling, and racing — 5K to Marathon*

The road-running edition of the OSPREY Expert Coach program — same engine as the ultra, cycling, swimming, and rowing plans, rebuilt for the four classic road distances. Turns four onboarding answers into a personalized, periodized plan. Written for the athlete: plain language, real paces, zero fluff.

## 1. How the Expert Coach Program Works

Every OSPREY plan is built from four onboarding inputs.

| # | Onboarding Input | What it drives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Experience & current mileage — years, miles/week, sessions/week | Starting volume, progression rate |
| 2 | Goal race — 5K / 10K / Half / Marathon, and current threshold pace | Energy-system mix, key sessions, taper length |
| 3 | Timeline to race day — weeks until target | Macrocycle structure (Base/Build/Peak/Taper) |
| 4 | Constraints & injury history — schedule, surfaces, past injuries | Weekly session count, strength design, load caps |

**One-line philosophy:** Build the aerobic base, sharpen with the right blend of threshold, VO2 and speed for the distance, and arrive fresh.

## 2. Training Philosophy

All four distances are aerobic-dominant — even the 5K is ~95% aerobic — but the intensity emphasis shifts as the race shortens. Seiler polarized 80/20: ~80% easy, ~20% hard. 3:1 loading (three build weeks, one down week). Daniels VDOT zones with **threshold (T) pace as the anchor** — T ≈ 1-hour race pace, roughly 25–30 sec/mi slower than 5K pace. All other zones are set as offsets from T. Re-test threshold every 4–6 weeks.

| Macrocycle | Emphasis | Looks like |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Aerobic volume + strides | Easy mileage rising, strides, general strength |
| Build | Distance-specific quality | T / I / R work per the race, mileage peaks |
| Peak | Race-pace sharpening | Race-pace sessions, race-sim, volume easing |
| Taper | Freshness | Volume cut, intensity kept |

**Five-zone model (Daniels E/M/T/I/R):**

| Zone | Name | Pace vs anchor | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| E | Easy | Conversational, ~1–2 min/mi slower than T | Aerobic base, recovery |
| M | Marathon | ~T + 15–30 s/mi | Marathon-specific stamina |
| T | Threshold | anchor (1-hr race pace) | Lactate clearance, the workhorse |
| I | Interval / VO2 | ~T − 10 to −20 s/mi, 3–5 min reps | Aerobic power ceiling |
| R | Repetition | Faster than I, short reps + full rest | Speed, economy, sprint |

## 3. The Key Sessions

- **Easy runs + strides:** the aerobic bulk; strides (6–8×~20 s) keep the legs fast year-round.
- **Threshold:** tempo runs or cruise intervals (e.g. 3–5×8–12 min at T) — the highest-return session for every distance.
- **VO2 / intervals (I):** 5–6×3–5 min at I pace with equal-ish rest — raises the ceiling, biggest for 5K/10K.
- **Repetition (R):** short fast reps with full recovery — speed and economy, biggest for 5K.
- **Marathon-pace long runs / race-pace work:** distance-specific — MP blocks for the marathon, goal-pace segments for shorter races.

**Distance emphasis:**

| Distance | Primary systems | Signature work |
|---|---|---|
| Marathon (26.2) | Aerobic base + MP stamina | Long runs with MP blocks (MP = T + 15–30 s/mi) |
| Half (13.1) | Threshold-dominant | Long T intervals, HMP = T + 5–15 s/mi |
| 10K | Threshold + VO2 blend | T + I sessions, 10K pace = T − 5 to −15 s/mi |
| 5K | VO2-max dominant + speed | I + R work, 5K pace = T − 20 to −30 s/mi |

## 4. Form & Running Economy

| Element | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| Cadence | ~170–180 spm; avoid overstriding |
| Posture | Tall, slight forward lean from the ankles, relaxed shoulders |
| Footstrike | Under the center of mass, quick and light |
| Arms | Relaxed ~90°, driving back, no cross-body |

**Economy-first:** strides, drills and strength improve economy more than grinding junk miles. Good form holds up when fatigue hits late in the race.

## 5. Strength & Cross-Training

| Phase | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Movement quality + general strength | Squats, lunges, deadlifts, calf raises, core |
| Build | Max strength → power | Heavier lifts, single-leg, hops |
| Peak | Power / elasticity | Plyometrics, bounds, low-rep explosive |
| Taper | Maintenance + activation | Light, snappy, low volume |

**Priorities:** posterior chain, calves/Achilles resilience, single-leg stability, braced trunk. Twice-weekly strength + plyos is proven to improve running economy and cut injury risk. Cross-train (bike/pool) to add aerobic load with less impact when needed.

## 6. Nutrition & Hydration

| Day type | Carb target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Easy / recovery | 3–5 g/kg/day | Protein and veg high |
| Moderate | 5–7 g/kg/day | Everyday range |
| High volume / long | 8–10 g/kg/day | Big days, marathon builds |
| Race week (M/Half) | Carb-load to ~8–12 g/kg | Top glycogen |

**In-race carbs:** Marathon 60–90 g/hr; Half 30–60 g/hr only if racing 90+ min; **10K and 5K need no mid-race fuel** (top up beforehand). **Protein:** ~1.6–2.0 g/kg/day. **Hydration:** to thirst; sodium ~300–600+ mg/hr (~800 mg per litre of sweat); overdrinking → hyponatremia (<135 mmol/L). **Caffeine:** ~3 mg/kg pre-race helps. **Race day:** familiar breakfast 2–3 hrs out; carb-load the last 2–3 days for Marathon/Half. Nothing new on race day.

## 7. Race Preparation & Pacing

| Distance | Pacing | Warm-up | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Marathon | Even / slightly negative at MP; don't bank time | Short and easy | Fuel every 30–45 min; discipline early is everything |
| Half | Even at HMP, lift the last 5k | Moderate | One gel mid-race if 90+ min |
| 10K | Even/negative, hard from ~T−10 s/mi | Thorough (it hurts early) | No fuel; controlled first mile |
| 5K | Fast-controlled start, hold, empty the tank | Thorough (strides + build) | No fuel; mental toughness in the middle mile |

**Shared rule:** the shorter the race, the more warm-up and the earlier the discomfort; the longer the race, the more fueling and pacing discipline decide the result.

## 8. Taper & Race Day

Cut volume while keeping intensity — short race-pace touches keep the legs sharp.

| Distance | Taper length | Volume cut |
|---|---|---|
| Marathon | 3 weeks | ~25 / 25 / 30% across the weeks |
| Half | 1.5–2 weeks | Progressive |
| 10K | ~1.5 weeks | Progressive |
| 5K | ~1 week (sharpest) | Light, keep speed |

**Race day:** distance-appropriate warm-up, run your plan from the gun, break the race into segments, keep to the fueling schedule where it applies.

## 9. Staying Healthy — Load & Red Flags

The .01% difference is consecutive healthy weeks. Most running injuries are overuse from load spikes: mileage +10%/week cap, down week every 3–4 weeks (3:1). STOP/caution: **bone stress** (localized, worsening, point-tender — stop, assess); **tendon** (Achilles/patellar worsening with load — reduce, strengthen, check cadence); **systemic** (fatigue, high resting HR, poor sleep, stalling paces — down week). **Load rules:** intensity OR volume, not both; easy days truly easy; sleep + fuel; calcium and vitamin D for bone.

## 10. Four Runner Profiles

- **First marathon (intermediate, 4–5 runs/wk):** aerobic base + MP long-run blocks, threshold, disciplined fueling practice, 3-wk taper.
- **Half-marathon PR chaser (4–5 runs/wk):** threshold-dominant block, HMP work, moderate long runs, 1.5–2 wk taper.
- **10K racer (5–6 runs/wk):** T + I blend, thorough warm-ups, race-pace sharpening, ~1.5-wk taper.
- **5K speedster (busy, 3–5 runs/wk):** VO2 + R-pace speed, strides and plyos, sharp 1-wk taper, no in-race fuel.

**Illustrative threshold defaults used in the sample plans:** Marathon 7:10, Half 6:50, 10K 6:30, 5K 6:15 per mile.
