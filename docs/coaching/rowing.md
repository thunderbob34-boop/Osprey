# PROJECT OSPREY — The 2K Rowing Blueprint
*Your Expert Coach system for training, technique, and nutrition — start to finish line*

This is the rowing edition of the OSPREY Expert Coach program — the same engine behind the cycling, ultra, and swimming plans, rebuilt stroke-by-stroke for the 2,000-metre race. It turns four onboarding answers into a personalized, periodized plan that makes you faster without breaking your ribs or your back. Written for the athlete: plain language, real splits, zero fluff.

## 1. How the Expert Coach Program Works

Every OSPREY plan is built from four onboarding inputs.

| # | Onboarding Input | What it drives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Experience & current load — years rowing, meters/week, sessions/week | Starting volume, progression rate, technique vs. fitness emphasis |
| 2 | Goal race & boat class — 2k single/crew, sweep vs. scull, head races | Energy-system mix, race-pace sets, taper length |
| 3 | Timeline to peak regatta — weeks until target | Macrocycle structure (Base/Build/Peak weeks) |
| 4 | Constraints & injury history — schedule, water/erg access, ribs/low back | Weekly session count, strength design, load caps and red flags |

**One-line philosophy:** Build a big aerobic engine, sharpen it with race-specific power, protect the ribs and low back, and arrive at the regatta rested and sharp.

## 2. Training Philosophy

Rowing is aerobic-dominant power-endurance. The 2k lasts ~5:30–8:00 and is ~75–80% aerobic — but the top-end 20% wins races. Pyramidal distribution early (lots of low intensity, a threshold block, a little top-end), shifting polarized as the regatta nears — final ~6 weeks: low-intensity volume + hard Zone 3 = 90–95% of training.

| Macrocycle | Weeks out | Emphasis | Looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | Furthest | Aerobic capacity + technique | High UT2 volume, long steady rows, rate 18–22 |
| Build | Mid-season | Threshold → VO2 max | AT/TR intervals appear, strength peaks, rate creeps up |
| Peak | Weeks before | VO2 + race pace | Lower volume, race-rate pieces, broken 2ks |
| Taper | Final 1–2 wks | Freshness + sharpness | Volume cut 40–50%, intensity kept, full recovery |

**2k anchor / training splits** — paces set as offsets from current 2k split (/500m in a 2,000m test). Re-test every 4–6 weeks. Rowing's FTP/CSS equivalent.

| Zone | Name | Split vs 2k /500m | Rate (spm) | % 2k power | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| UT2 | Utilisation 2 | +12–16s slower | 18–20 | 55–65% | Aerobic base, recovery, technique |
| UT1 | Utilisation 1 | +6–10s slower | 22–24 | 65–75% | Build the engine, economy |
| AT | Anaerobic Threshold | +3–5s slower | 26–28 | 75–85% | Lactate clearance, race stamina |
| TR | Transport / VO2 | +0–2s slower | 28–32 | 85–95% | Max oxygen uptake |
| AN | Anaerobic | at/faster than 2k | 34–40 | 95–110% | Race pace, start, sprint, power |

Most weekly meters live in UT2/UT1. Hard 15–20% (AT/TR/AN) wins the race — kept genuinely hard, not a mushy middle. **Race-pace (sparingly):** race-rate pieces (2k pace 34–38 spm) — Build/Peak tool (broken 2ks, 4×500, starts), not the whole program.

## 3. The Key Sessions

- **Aerobic base:** long steady rows 60–90+ min UT2 rate 18–20; 3×20–30 min UT2 blocks. Biggest driver of 2k speed.
- **Threshold/steady-state:** 4×8–10 min AT (rate 24–26, 3–5 min rest); 3×2k UT1/AT.
- **VO2/transport:** 6–8×3–4 min TR (1:1 rest); 5×1k; 8×500 full recovery.
- **Race-pace/anaerobic:** broken 2ks (4×500 or 8×250), race starts (5+15 high), 4×2 min race rate. Stop when the split slips.
- **Technique & ratio:** drills every session (pause, legs-only, feet-out, square blades, ratio) + low-rate technical rows.

## 4. Technique & Stroke Mechanics

| Phase | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| Catch | Quick quiet blade entry — place, don't slam. No aggressive "attack." |
| Drive | Legs first, then swing back open, then arms. Sequence, don't shoot the slide. |
| Finish | Clean level handle to body; tap down and away before knees rise. |
| Recovery | Hands away, body over from hips, then knees — ratio ~2× the drive. |

**Ribs-and-back-first:** rate follows length and ratio, not muscling the slide. Limit sweep trunk rotation ~5–10° (over-rotation → rib stress). Rib/low-back pain is a STOP signal.

## 5. Strength & Cross-Training

| Phase | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Movement quality + general strength | Squats, deadlifts, rows, pull-ups, core, hip mobility |
| Build | Max strength → power | Trap-bar deadlift, front squat, weighted pulls, step-ups |
| Peak | Power / rate of force | Explosive low-rep lifts, jumps, med-ball, starts |
| Taper | Maintenance + activation | Light, snappy, low-volume |

**Priorities:** serratus/scapular strength (protect ribs), leg extensors, braced trunk. Weekly non-negotiable: core anti-rotation, hip flexibility, posterior chain. Cross-train (bike/run/swim) to carry aerobic load without compression when ribs are grumbly.

## 6. Nutrition & Hydration

| Day type | Carb target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Easy / technique / low volume | 3–5 g/kg/day | Keep protein and veg high |
| Moderate volume + intensity | 5–7 g/kg/day | Everyday range |
| High volume / doubles | 8–10 g/kg/day | Big aerobic days, two-a-days |
| Very high / peak week | 10–12 g/kg/day | Heaviest blocks only |

**Protein:** ~1.6–2.0 g/kg/day, ~0.3 g/kg per meal, dose within 30–60 min of hard sessions. **Around training:** carb-forward meal 3–4h before (~1–2 g/kg, low fat/fibre); recovery within 30–60 min = carbs + 20–40 g protein. **Hydration:** ~35–45 mL/kg/day baseline; 2% loss slows you; 5–8% carb drink w/ sodium on long/hot rows; don't over-drink (hyponatremia <135 mmol/L). **Regatta day:** 2k too short to fuel mid-race — arrive topped-up; full meal 3–4h out; between heats sip fluid+sodium, graze 30–60 g carbs/hr. Nothing new on race day.

## 7. Race Preparation by Boat & Crew

| Race | Demand | Training emphasis | Race strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2k single (1x) | Aerobic power + self-pacing | UT/AT volume, race-rate 500s, starts | Even/slightly negative split; own rhythm |
| 2k sweep crew (2-/4-/8+) | Aerobic power + crew rhythm | Rate/ratio matching, race-pace together | Trust the call; swing as one; commit to sprint |
| Lightweight 2k | Power-to-weight + aerobic | High aerobic vol, disciplined weight mgmt | Efficient stroke, patient middle 1k, brave last 500 |
| Head race (4–6k) | Aerobic engine + rate control | High UT1/AT vol, steady-state, steering | Metronomic rate, negative-split, hunt crews |
| Masters sprints (1k) | Aerobic-anaerobic blend | Race-pace 250s, threshold, recovery | Fast controlled start, hold form, strong close |

**2k shape:** ~45s start + 4–6 min near-max + 45–60s sprint. Elite go ~103% avg speed first 500, settle ~98–99% middle, lift at end. Blow the first 500 → third 500 ("valley of death") buries you.

## 8. Taper & Race Day

Cut volume 40–50% over final 1–2 wks while KEEPING intensity and race-rate touches. Volume-down-only leaves speed on the dock.

| Lever | Do | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Volume | Reduce 40–50% | Sheds fatigue, restores force |
| Intensity | Keep it — short race-rate pieces | Maintains feel and boat speed |
| Frequency | Keep most sessions, shorter | Preserves rhythm and blade feel |
| Duration | Sprints ~1 wk; longer/masters ~1–2 wks | Bigger events shed more fatigue |

**Race day:** full warm-up (10–15 min UT2, build 500s, race-rate strokes, practice start, stay warm); fuel light/familiar 2–3h before; own the plan (start, settle, base, sprint); between prelims/finals spin easy, refuel, re-warm.

## 9. Staying Healthy — Load & Red Flags

.01% difference = consecutive healthy weeks. Two signature injuries: rib stress fractures (8–16% of elite rowers) + low-back pain — both from load spikes and technique breakdown. Progress ≤~10% meters/wk. STOP signals: **rib** (localized chest-wall pain, worse on drive/deep breath — see clinician, don't row through); **low back** (sharp/worsening, esp. rounded catch — cut load, fix sequencing/hip mobility, core stability); **systemic** (fatigue, high resting HR, poor sleep, stalling splits — recovery week); **form-altering pain** (fix the cause). **Load rules:** gradual build + recovery wk every 3–4; intensity OR volume, not both; honest rate (high-rate on a rounded back breaks ribs); erg on slides/floating-head; calcium + vitamin D.

## 10. Four Rower Profiles

- **Returning Adult** (3 rows/wk, 2k single): rebuild sequence + aerobic base; +10%/wk, recovery every 4th; heavy technique + UT2; 2k test wk 4.
- **Junior Sweep Rower** (5–6 rows/wk, 2k 8+): base + race-rate + crew rhythm; broken 2ks and starts; power strength; 1–2 wk taper; monitor ribs/back on spikes.
- **Masters Head Racer** (40s+, 4 rows/wk, 5k head): high UT1/AT vol, economy, steering/rate control; metronomic pacing; posterior-chain + core prehab non-negotiable.
- **Lightweight Racer** (college, 8+ sess/wk, lwt 2k): disciplined power-to-weight; high aerobic vol + sane weight mgmt; full periodization; peaks with broken 2ks; rigorous rib/back prehab.
