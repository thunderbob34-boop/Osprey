# PROJECT OSPREY — The Cycling Blueprint
*Your Expert Coach system for training, fueling, and racing — base miles to the finish line sprint*

This is the cycling edition of the OSPREY Expert Coach program — the same engine behind the ultra, swimming, and rowing plans, rebuilt around the pedal stroke and the power meter. It turns four onboarding answers into a personalized, periodized plan that makes you faster on the road and in the bunch. Written for the athlete: plain language, real watts, zero fluff. Discipline focus: road racing / criterium.

## 1. How the Expert Coach Program Works

Every OSPREY plan is built from four onboarding inputs.

| # | Onboarding Input | What it drives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Experience & FTP — years riding, hrs/week, current FTP (W and W/kg) | Starting volume, zones, progression rate |
| 2 | Goal event & demands — crit, road race, TT, gran fondo | Energy-system mix, race-specific sets, taper length |
| 3 | Timeline to peak event — weeks until target | Macrocycle structure (Base/Build/Peak/Taper) |
| 4 | Constraints & injury history — schedule, indoor/outdoor, knees/back | Weekly session count, strength design, load caps |

**One-line philosophy:** Build a deep aerobic base, raise FTP with threshold and sweet-spot work, add race-winning power on top, and arrive at the event fresh and sharp.

## 2. Training Philosophy

Road/crit racing is aerobic-base with a supra-threshold finish. Polarized 80/20: ~80% easy endurance (Zone 2), ~20% genuinely hard, minimal junk-tempo. Base builds the engine; specificity wins races. 3:1 loading: three build weeks, one recovery week.

| Macrocycle | Weeks out | Emphasis | Looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | Furthest | Aerobic volume + efficiency | Long Z2 rides, sweet spot appears, general strength |
| Build | Mid | Threshold → VO2 max | Threshold + VO2 intervals, strength peaks |
| Peak | Weeks before | Race power + specificity | Crit surges, sprints, race-sim, lower volume |
| Taper | Final 1–2 wks | Freshness | Volume cut, intensity kept, legs sharpened |

**FTP anchor / power zones** — everything is set as a % of FTP (functional threshold power). Re-test FTP every 6–8 weeks. Coggan 7-zone model.

| Zone | Name | % FTP | Feel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Active recovery | <55% | Very easy spin | Recovery, flush |
| 2 | Endurance | 56–75% | All-day pace | Aerobic base — the bulk of training |
| 3 | Tempo | 76–90% | Comfortably hard | Aerobic strength (used purposefully) |
| 4 | Threshold | 91–105% | Hard, sustainable ~1 hr | Raise FTP |
| 5 | VO2 max | 106–120% | Very hard, 3–5 min | Aerobic power ceiling |
| 6 | Anaerobic | 121–150% | Sprint-ish, 30–60 s | Crit surges, attacks |
| 7 | Neuromuscular | Max | All-out, <15 s | Sprint, jump |

Sweet spot (~88–94% FTP) is the efficiency workhorse that lifts FTP with manageable fatigue. Most miles live in Z2; the hard work (Z4–7) is what wins.

## 3. The Key Sessions

- **Endurance (Z2):** long steady rides, the foundation — builds fat-burning and durability. The bulk of weekly hours.
- **Sweet spot / threshold:** 3–4×10–20 min at 88–105% FTP — the highest-return FTP builder. Race stamina in the legs.
- **VO2 max:** 5–6×3–5 min at 106–120% FTP (equal rest) — raises the aerobic ceiling; makes threshold feel easy.
- **Anaerobic / sprint — "crit weapons":** repeated 30–60 s surges >120% FTP and 5–10 s max sprints, over a big Z2 base — the ability to answer attacks and win the dash.
- **Strength:** leg-focused gym work (see §5).

## 4. Position, Pedaling & Efficiency

| Element | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| Pedal stroke | Smooth round application, no dead spot; cadence ~85–95 rpm on the flats |
| Position | Aero and sustainable; stable core, quiet upper body |
| Cornering (crit) | Look through the corner, weight the outside pedal, exit on the power |
| Pack skills | Hold a wheel, close gaps smoothly, conserve in the draft (~25–30% saving) |

**Efficiency-first:** a smooth stroke and good draft management save more watts than raw power. Crit racing is 90% positioning and 10% brute force — spend matches wisely.

## 5. Strength & Cross-Training

| Phase | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Movement quality + general strength | Squats, deadlifts, lunges, step-ups, core |
| Build | Max strength → power | Heavier squats, trap-bar deadlift, single-leg |
| Peak | Power / sprint force | Explosive low-rep, jumps, standing starts |
| Taper | Maintenance + activation | Light, snappy, low volume |

**Priorities:** leg extensors and glutes (sprint and climbing power), braced trunk for the aero position, hip mobility, single-leg balance. Twice-weekly strength protects the knees and low back and adds sprint watts. Cross-train sparingly — the bike is specific.

## 6. Nutrition & Hydration

| Day type | Carb target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Easy / recovery | 3–5 g/kg/day | Keep protein and veg high |
| Moderate volume | 5–7 g/kg/day | Everyday range |
| Long / hard days | 8–10 g/kg/day | Big endurance or interval days |
| Peak / stage-block | 10–12 g/kg/day | Heaviest weeks |

**In-ride carbs by duration/intensity:** 30–60 g/hr for shorter/steady, 60–90 g/hr for long or hard, up to 90–120 g/hr for very long/racing with a trained gut (glucose:fructose ~1:0.8 to 2:1). **Protein:** ~1.6–2.0 g/kg/day; ~20–40 g in recovery within 30–60 min. **Hydration:** ~500–1000 mL/hr depending on heat; sodium 600–1000 mg/hr (~800 mg per litre of sweat; +25% for salty sweaters, +150 mg in heat). Overdrinking dilutes sodium → hyponatremia (<135 mmol/L). **Heat:** drop carbs ~10–15 g/hr, add fluid + sodium, acclimate 10–14 days. **Race day:** top up before, fuel from early, use bottles and food you've practiced. Nothing new on race day.

## 7. Race Preparation by Event

| Race | Demand | Training emphasis | Race strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Criterium | Repeated supra-threshold surges + sprint | Anaerobic repeats, sprints, cornering, big Z2 base | Position at the front, conserve in the draft, spend matches late, commit to the sprint |
| Road race | Aerobic base + selective efforts + finish | Long Z2, threshold/VO2, race-sim, sprint | Sit in, cover key moves, fuel all race, time the finale |
| Time trial | Sustained threshold power | Threshold/sweet spot, aero position work | Even/negative pace at target watts, aero discipline |
| Gran fondo | Deep endurance + climbing | High volume, sweet spot, long climbs, fueling | Steady pacing, eat/drink on schedule, ride within limits |

**Crit shape:** it's a series of accelerations, not a steady effort — repeated jumps out of corners over a big aerobic base, decided by a sprint. Being at the front costs less than fighting from the back.

## 8. Taper & Race Day

Cut volume over the final 1–2 weeks while keeping intensity — short threshold and race-power touches keep the legs sharp. Volume-down-only leaves speed on the table.

| Lever | Do | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Volume | Reduce over 1–2 wks | Sheds fatigue, restores force |
| Intensity | Keep short quality touches | Maintains sharpness and power |
| Frequency | Keep most rides, shorter | Preserves rhythm and feel |
| Duration | Stage races/fondos taper longer; crits shorter | Bigger events shed more fatigue |

**Race day:** full warm-up (especially crits/TTs — build efforts + a few openers); fuel light and familiar 2–3 hrs before; ride your plan; recover, refuel and re-warm between heats or stages.

## 9. Staying Healthy — Load & Red Flags

The .01% difference is consecutive healthy weeks. Cycling injuries are mostly overuse and position-related: knees (patellofemoral, from fit/cadence/load spikes), low back and neck (position), and overtraining. Progress load ≤~10%/week, recovery week every 3–4 weeks (3:1). STOP/caution: **knee pain** (check fit, saddle height, cleat position; reduce load, raise cadence, strengthen); **low-back/neck** (position + core work); **systemic** (fatigue, high resting HR, poor sleep, stalling FTP — down week). **Load rules:** intensity OR volume, not both; build gradually; sleep and fuel the training; get a proper bike fit.

## 10. Four Rider Profiles

- **New racer (2–3 rides/wk, first crit season):** build Z2 base, add sweet spot, learn cornering and pack skills; conservative progression; practice fueling on long rides.
- **Intermediate crit racer (5–8 hrs/wk):** big Z2 base + anaerobic repeats + sprints, threshold/VO2 to raise FTP, full periodization, 1-wk sharpening taper.
- **Time-trial specialist (structured, 6–10 hrs/wk):** threshold/sweet-spot dominant, aero position work, even-paced race-sim, longer taper.
- **Gran fondo rider (masters, 4–6 rides/wk):** high endurance volume + climbing, sweet spot, disciplined fueling, extra recovery, prehab for knees/back.

**Next step:** personalize with current weekly hours + FTP, real goal event & date, and injury history.
