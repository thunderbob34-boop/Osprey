# PROJECT OSPREY — The Hyrox Blueprint
*8 km of running • 8 functional stations — race the fitness race faster*

This is the Hyrox edition of the OSPREY Expert Coach program — the endurance engine rebuilt for the sled, the ski erg and the wall ball. It turns four onboarding answers into a periodized plan that holds your run pace under fatigue and gets you through every station without redlining.

## 1. How the Expert Coach Program Works

| # | Onboarding Input | What it drives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Experience & current fitness — training age, recent 5K / running threshold, sessions/week | Volume, run-vs-strength emphasis, progression rate |
| 2 | Goal division & target time — Open / Pro / Doubles, sub-90 / sub-75 / sub-60 | Pacing targets, station focus, specificity |
| 3 | Timeline to race — weeks until race day | Macrocycle: Base / Build / Peak / Taper |
| 4 | Constraints & injury history — schedule, equipment access, knees / back / shoulders | Session count, movement selection, load caps |

**One-line philosophy:** Build an aerobic engine that holds pace under load, get strong enough that the stations don't spike your heart rate, rehearse running on tired legs, and pace it like a race — not eight separate efforts.

## 2. Training Philosophy

Hyrox is a compromised-running race: your run splits are decided by how much the stations cost you. Training is polarized — most running easy/aerobic to build the engine, with sharp threshold and race-pace work — layered with strength-endurance so the sled, lunges and wall balls barely raise your heart rate.

**The race — 8 runs, 8 stations, in fixed order**

| Order | Run | Station | Distance / Reps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1 km | SkiErg | 1000 m |
| 2 | 1 km | Sled Push | 50 m (2×25) |
| 3 | 1 km | Sled Pull | 50 m (2×25) |
| 4 | 1 km | Burpee Broad Jump | 80 m |
| 5 | 1 km | Rowing | 1000 m |
| 6 | 1 km | Farmers Carry | 200 m |
| 7 | 1 km | Sandbag Lunges | 100 m |
| 8 | 1 km | Wall Balls | 100 reps |
| Finish | — | — | 8 km run total + roxzone transitions |

**Station weights by division (kg)**

| Station | Open M | Open W | Pro M | Pro W |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sled Push (incl. sled) | 152 | 102 | 202 | 152 |
| Sled Pull (incl. sled) | 103 | 78 | 153 | 103 |
| Farmers Carry (each hand) | 24 | 16 | 32 | 24 |
| Sandbag Lunges | 20 | 10 | 30 | 20 |
| Wall Balls | 6 kg / 10 ft | 4 kg / 9 ft | 9 kg / 10 ft | 6 kg / 9 ft |

Wall balls are 100 reps in every division; only ball weight and target height change.

**Macrocycle**

| Phase | Weeks out | Emphasis | Looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | Furthest | Aerobic base + strength foundation | Z2 run volume, general strength, station technique |
| Build | Mid | Threshold + strength-endurance + stations | Tempo/threshold runs, station intervals, compromised running |
| Peak | Near | Race specificity | Half & full simulations, pacing, roxzone practice |
| Taper | Final 1–2 wks | Freshness | Volume down, short race-pace + station touches |

**Running zones (anchored to threshold pace)**

| Zone | Name | Effort vs threshold | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Z1 | Easy/Recovery | +45–75 s/km | Recovery, easy volume |
| Z2 | Aerobic base | +25–45 s/km | The engine — bulk of running |
| Z3 | Threshold / race | ±10 s/km | Race-specific, sustainable hard |
| Z4 | VO2 max | −10 to −25 s/km | Top-end, 3–5 min reps |
| Z5 | Sprint/anaerobic | All-out | Finishing kick, sled & burpee surges |

Realistic Hyrox run pace sits near threshold; because stations pre-fatigue you, plan compromised-run splits at ~threshold + 15–30 s/km.

## 3. The Key Sessions

- **Compromised-running intervals** — signature session: e.g. 1 km race pace → 50 m sled push → 1 km race pace, repeated.
- **Threshold / tempo runs** — sustained Z3.
- **Long aerobic Z2 run** — builds the engine cheaply and safely.
- **Station strength-endurance** — sled repeats, wall balls in sets of 25–50, lunge volume, 1000 m ski/row intervals at target split.
- **Race simulations** — half then full sims in the peak block.
- **Grip & core** — farmers holds/carries and trunk work.

## 4. Technique & Movement Standards

SkiErg full-body hinge/lat pull (don't over-pull early); sleds low body angle, short powerful steps / hand-over-hand hinge; burpee broad jump minimal & rhythmic; row/ski settle on a repeatable split; farmers carry braced/tall, don't set the bells down; sandbag lunges controlled and patient; wall balls pre-plan the break (e.g. 10×10 or 5×20).

## 5. Strength & Supporting Work

| Phase | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Movement quality + general strength | Squat, deadlift, lunge, press, pull, sled |
| Build | Max strength → strength-endurance | Heavy lower + high-rep station-specific circuits |
| Peak | Maintenance + specificity | Reduced lifting, station touches only |

## 6. Nutrition & Hydration

| Target | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Carbohydrate | 5–8 g/kg/day | Fuels run volume + strength |
| Protein | 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day | Recovery from concurrent training |
| In-race carbs | 30–60 g/hr if racing >75 min | Gel/drink |
| Sodium | 500–1000 mg/hr | Scale to sweat; more in heat |
| Caffeine | 3–6 mg/kg pre-race | Familiar dose only |

Carb-load, familiar carb-forward meal 2–3 h before, sip to thirst, nothing new race day. Over-drinking risks hyponatremia (<135 mmol/L).

## 7. Competition Preparation

Race it as one effort split across sixteen segments. The opening SkiErg–Sled Push–Sled Pull block is the cardiovascular crux — go controlled. Build a pacing plan: target run split, target station time, fixed wall-ball break scheme. Rehearse the roxzone (transition corridor) — seconds add up across eight transitions.

## 8. Taper & Competition Day

Cut volume 40–50% over the final 1–2 weeks, keep short race-pace runs + light station touches. Race morning: familiar carbs 2–3 h out, brief run + station primer, commit to the opening-run pace even though it feels easy.

## 9. Staying Healthy — Load & Red Flags

Run volume too fast (shin/Achilles/knee — cap ~10%/wk); low-back from sleds/lunges/sandbag (check bracing, don't round under fatigue); shoulder from ski/wall balls; grip/forearm overuse. Recovery week every 4th; add intensity or volume, rarely both.

## 10. Athlete Profiles

- **The Runner Crossing Over** (big engine, weak stations) — build sled/lunge/wall-ball strength and grip, learn to run under load.
- **The Strength Athlete / CrossFitter** (strong stations, weak run) — build Z2 run volume/threshold, run economy, compromised running.
- **The First-Timer, Open** (sub-90 goal) — balanced base, learn stations & roxzone, finish strong.
- **The Competitive Pro** (sub-70) — high volume, precise pacing, station efficiency, repeated race simulations.
