# PROJECT OSPREY — The Powerlifting Blueprint
*Squat • Bench • Deadlift — build a bigger total without breaking down*

This is the powerlifting edition of the OSPREY Expert Coach program — the same engine that powers the endurance plans, rebuilt around the barbell. It turns four onboarding answers into a periodized plan that adds kilos to your total and lands you healthy on the platform. Written for the lifter: plain language, real numbers, zero fluff.

## 1. How the Expert Coach Program Works

| # | Onboarding Input | What it drives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Experience & current strength — years lifting, current squat/bench/deadlift 1RMs (or estimated), sessions/week | Starting volume, rate of load progression, technique vs. load emphasis |
| 2 | Goal — first meet, weight-class total, or peak strength; federation & timing | Block emphasis, specificity, length of the peak |
| 3 | Timeline to meet — weeks until platform day | Macrocycle: Accumulation / Intensification / Peak / Taper |
| 4 | Constraints & injury history — schedule, equipment, low back / shoulder / knee / hip | Weekly frequency, exercise selection, load caps and red flags |

**One-line philosophy:** Build strength on a base of quality volume, express it with heavy specific practice, peak fatigue-free, and lift a big total without breaking.

## 2. Training Philosophy

Intensity is autoregulated. Percentage of 1RM sets the plan; RPE (rate of perceived exertion) and RIR (reps in reserve) keep it honest, because your true max moves 5–15% day to day with sleep, stress and fatigue. Prescriptions are written as "%1RM at a target RPE" — if the bar is heavier than the RPE allows, you hold or drop 2–3% rather than grind.

**Macrocycle — block periodization**

| Block | Weeks out | Emphasis | Load / reps | Looks like |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Accumulation | Furthest | Volume, work capacity, muscle | 65–80%, 5–12 reps, RIR 2–4 | Higher sets, variations, back-off volume |
| Intensification | Mid | Max strength | 80–90%, 3–6 reps, RIR 1–2 | Comp lifts, fewer reps, moderate volume |
| Peak | Near | Realize the 1RM, specificity | 88–95%+, 1–3 reps, RIR 0–1 | Heavy singles/doubles, opener rehearsal |
| Taper | Final week | Freshness | Openers only, light | Shed fatigue, keep the groove |

**Intensity zones (% of 1RM)**

| Zone | %1RM | Reps | RPE | RIR | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hypertrophy | 65–75% | 6–12 | 6–8 | 2–4 | Muscle, work capacity, connective tissue |
| Strength-Volume | 75–85% | 3–6 | 7–8 | 2–3 | The bulk of raw strength gains |
| Max Strength | 85–92% | 1–3 | 8–9 | 1–2 | Neural drive, comp specificity |
| Peak / Test | 93–100%+ | 1 | 9–10 | 0–1 | Realize and express the max |
| Speed / Dynamic | 40–60% | 1–3 | — | — | Rate of force, bar-speed technique |

Prilepin's table is the volume guardrail: ~70% → 3–6 reps/set, 12–24 total; ~80% → 2–4 reps/set, 10–20 total; ~90% → 1–2 reps/set, 4–10 total.

## 3. The Key Sessions

- **Competition lifts** — squat, bench, deadlift to a top set at a prescribed RPE, then back-off volume (e.g. top single @8, then 3×3 at ~85% of that load).
- **Variation work** — pause squat, tempo squat, deficit/paused deadlift, close-grip / Spoto / Larsen bench.
- **Speed / dynamic effort (optional)** — 40–60% moved with maximal intent.
- **Accessory hypertrophy** — rows, pull-ups, dips, leg press, RDLs/hamstrings, triceps, upper back, abs.
- **Re-tests** — a heavy single or estimated 1RM at the end of each block to re-anchor loads.

## 4. Technique & Movement Standards

- **Squat** — 360° brace on a big breath, bar over midfoot, knees track over toes, hips break below the knee crease (legal depth), controlled descent, drive the floor away.
- **Bench** — shoulder blades retracted and depressed, upper-back and leg drive, tight bar path to lower chest, a visible pause on the chest for competition.
- **Deadlift** — bar over midfoot, lats set, brace before the pull, push the floor away and finish with hips (conventional or sumo).
- **Autoregulate by bar speed** — if speed tanks below your RPE target, the set is done. Never train through form breakdown under maximal load.

## 5. Strength & Supporting Work

| Phase | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Off-season | Hypertrophy + weak points | High-volume accessories, variations, GPP |
| Pre-meet | Maintain size, sharpen the comp lifts | Comp lifts + targeted accessories |
| Peak | Specificity | Minimal accessories, keep the joints fresh |

Conditioning: 2–3 easy low-intensity cardio sessions a week aids recovery/body comp and doesn't blunt strength when sub-threshold. Non-negotiable prehab: hip/thoracic mobility, upper-back volume, rotator-cuff/abdominal bracing.

## 6. Nutrition & Hydration

| Target | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Carbohydrate | 4–7 g/kg/day | Higher on high-volume days |
| Protein | 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day | ~0.3 g/kg per meal across 4–5 meals |
| Fat | 0.8–1.5 g/kg/day | Supports hormones; fills remaining calories |
| Energy | Slight surplus (off-season) / maintenance (peak) | Surplus to build, maintenance to keep leverages |

Creatine monohydrate 3–5 g/day; caffeine 3–6 mg/kg pre-session/meet. Making weight: settle into class months out; if cutting, keep acute water cut ≤2–4% BW and only with a 24-hour weigh-in; rehydrate/refuel hard between weigh-in and platform.

## 7. Competition Preparation

Nine attempts win the meet — three each in squat, bench, deadlift. Choose them to go 9-for-9 on total.

| Attempt | % of goal 3rd | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Opener (1st) | ~89–91% | A weight you could triple today |
| Second | ~95–96% | A strong single that sets up the third |
| Third | ~100–102% | Your max expression on the day |

Attempt jumps: squat/deadlift 5–7.5%, bench 3–5%. IPF Worlds data: lifters making all three opened ~91%, hit ~96% on the second.

## 8. Taper & Competition Day

Cut volume 40–60% over the final 1–2 weeks, keep intensity via opener-range singles. Last genuinely heavy session ~7–10 days out; final week light and snappy with opener touches 3–4 days out. Day: familiar carb-forward breakfast, caffeine timed to first lift, warm up off the opener, follow the attempt card by bar speed, refuel/rehydrate between lifts.

## 9. Staying Healthy — Load & Red Flags

STOP or modify on: sharp localized low-back pain (vs. fatigue); shoulder pain in bench/pressing; knee/hip pain altering the squat pattern; bar speed & top-end strength falling week over week with poor sleep (= overreaching). Load rules: small %/RPE steps, autoregulate every session, deload every 4–6 weeks, rarely true-max in training.

## 10. Athlete Profiles

- **The Novice** (2–3×/wk, first meet ~16 wks out) — linear progression, technique-first, moderate %, simple 2-week peak.
- **The Intermediate Raw Lifter** (weight-class total goal) — block periodization + DUP, RPE autoregulation, 12-week peak.
- **The Masters Lifter** (40s+, joint history) — more recovery, submaximal volume, joint-sparing variations.
- **The Advanced Competitor** (national-qualifier hunt) — block/conjugate hybrid, precise peaking, weak-point work, weight management.
