# PROJECT OSPREY — The Triathlon Blueprint
*Your Expert Coach system for training, nutrition, and race-day execution — Sprint to Ironman*

This is the triathlon edition of the OSPREY Expert Coach program — the same engine that powers the swim, bike, run, row, and ultra plans, rebuilt for the three-sport athlete. It turns four onboarding answers into a personalized, periodized plan that makes you faster across swim, bike, and run — and through the two transitions that quietly decide races — without breaking you in the process. Written for the athlete: plain language, real numbers, zero fluff.

## 1. How the Expert Coach Program Works

Every OSPREY plan is built from four onboarding inputs. Answer honestly and the engine maps you onto the right volume split, intensity mix, and race approach.

| # | Onboarding Input | What it drives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Experience & current load — years in each sport, weekly hours, sessions/week, swim CSS / bike FTP / run threshold | Starting volume, per-sport emphasis, progression rate |
| 2 | Goal race & distance — Sprint / Olympic / 70.3 / Ironman, course profile | Energy-system mix, brick design, fueling plan, taper length |
| 3 | Timeline to A-race — weeks until target | Macrocycle structure (Base/Build/Peak/Taper) |
| 4 | Constraints & injury history — schedule, pool/road/trail access, limiter sport, injuries | Weekly session count, discipline balance, load caps, red flags |

**One-line philosophy:** Build three durable engines, spend the most fitness where you lose the most time, master the transitions and the bike-to-run, and arrive fresh, fueled, and rehearsed.

**The triathlon truth:** you don't train three sports — you train one athlete across three sports. Fatigue, fueling, and recovery are shared. The strongest single-sport athlete rarely wins; the athlete who runs well off the bike does.

## 2. Training Philosophy

**Intensity distribution — polarized, pooled across sports.** Roughly 80% of total weekly training time is easy aerobic (Z1–Z2) and ~20% is genuinely hard (Z4–Z5), counted across all three sports combined — not per sport. Early season leans pyramidal (more threshold work); as the A-race nears it polarizes and gets race-specific.

| Macrocycle | Weeks out | Emphasis | Looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | Furthest | Aerobic capacity + technique in all 3 | High easy volume, drills, swim CSS, bike endurance, run economy |
| Build | Mid-season | Threshold → race-specific | Race-pace bricks appear, sustained threshold, strength peaks |
| Peak | Weeks before | VO2 + race intensity | Lower volume, sharp race-simulation bricks, key sessions only |
| Taper | Final 1–3 wks | Freshness + feel | Volume cut 40–60%, intensity & openers kept, full recovery |

**Discipline weighting — spend time where you lose it.** The bike is the biggest time chunk and sets up the run; the run off the bike is where races are won or lost; the swim is the smallest time share but a bad swim can wreck the day. A common weekly split: **swim ~20%, bike ~50%, run ~30%** of hours — then bias toward your limiter.

**Your three anchors (test each, re-test every 4–8 wks):**
- **Swim — CSS (Critical Swim Speed):** all-out 400, full rest, all-out 200. `CSS/100 = (400s − 200s) ÷ 2`. Sets all pool paces.
- **Bike — FTP (Functional Threshold Power):** ~95% of 20-min max, or a ramp test. Sets 7 power zones.
- **Run — Threshold pace / LTHR:** ~1-hr race pace, or 30-min TT (avg HR of last 20 min = LTHR). Sets run zones.

**The five effort zones (applied per sport off its own anchor):**

| Zone | Name | Effort | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Z1 | Recovery | Very easy, conversational | Active recovery, technique |
| Z2 | Aerobic/Endurance | Easy, all-day pace | Build the engine, fat oxidation, durability |
| Z3 | Tempo/Sweet Spot | Comfortably hard | Race-specific endurance (esp. long-course) |
| Z4 | Threshold | Hard, ~1-hr limit | Raise the ceiling on sustainable pace/power |
| Z5 | VO2/Anaerobic | Very hard, minutes | Max oxygen uptake, top-end, surges |

## 3. The Key Sessions — By Discipline

**Swim**
- Aerobic/threshold: CSS sets (e.g. 10–20×100 @ CSS on 10–15s rest); descending 200s.
- Open-water skills: sighting every 4–6 strokes, drafting, pack starts, deep-water starts.
- Speed/feel: short reps 1:1, broken race swims at goal pace.
- Every session: drills (catch, body line, bilateral breathing) — economy is free speed.

**Bike**
- Endurance (Z2): the backbone; long rides build durability and fat use.
- Sweet spot / threshold: 2–4×10–20 min @ 88–100% FTP — the long-course workhorse.
- VO2max: 4–6×3–5 min @ 106–120% FTP — raises the ceiling.
- Race-position work: sustained efforts in aero/on the TT bars; long-course = hold power AND position.

**Run**
- Easy aerobic (Z2): most run volume; build here, protect the legs.
- Threshold/tempo: 20–40 min steady, or cruise intervals (e.g. 4–6×5 min @ threshold).
- VO2/speed: 5–8×3 min hard, or hills for economy and strength.
- Long run: aerobic distance for durability (crucial for 70.3/IM run).

**Brick sessions (bike→run, the signature triathlon workout)**
- Teach the legs to run off the bike; start short (10–15 min run off a ride) and build.
- Race-simulation brick in Build/Peak: ride at goal race power, then run at goal race pace off it.
- Frequency: ~1 quality brick/week; not every ride needs a run — bricks add fatigue.

## 4. Transitions & Open-Water Skills — Free Time

Transitions (T1 swim→bike, T2 bike→run) are the "fourth discipline." They cost nothing in fitness and can save 1–3+ minutes for free.

- **T1 (swim→bike):** wetsuit strip while running, goggles/cap off, helmet ON before touching the bike, mount line technique. Practice wetsuit removal at home.
- **T2 (bike→run):** dismount at speed, rack, shoes/race belt/visor, run out. Elastic laces and pre-clipped bike shoes save fumbling.
- **Setup:** minimal, repeatable transition layout; know your row/rack; rehearse the flow before race day.
- **Open water:** sighting, drafting (legal in swim — sit on feet/hip), straight-line swimming, mass-start positioning, exiting and running to T1.
- **Bike-to-run legs:** the first 1–2 km off the bike feel jelly-legged — practice it so race day feels normal, not alarming.

## 5. Strength & Durability

Periodized alongside the three sports: general strength in Base, max strength/power in Build, maintenance in Peak/Taper. 2×/week is plenty for most; it builds durability, economy, and injury resistance — not bulk.

| Phase | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Movement quality + general strength | Squats, hinges, lunges, rows, pull-ups, core, single-leg |
| Build | Max strength → power | Trap-bar deadlift, weighted step-ups, med-ball, hip thrusts |
| Peak | Power / rate of force | Explosive low-rep lifts, jumps, plyos (small doses) |
| Taper | Maintenance + activation | Light, snappy, low-volume; add zero fatigue |

**Non-negotiable prehab:** posterior chain (glutes/hamstrings), calf/Achilles loading for runners, rotator-cuff and scapular work for swimmers, hip and thoracic mobility. Single-leg strength protects the run.

## 6. Nutrition & Hydration

**Daily carbohydrate (periodize to the day, not a fixed diet):**

| Day type | Carb target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Easy / recovery / low volume | 3–5 g/kg/day | Keep protein and veg high |
| Moderate volume + intensity | 5–7 g/kg/day | Everyday range |
| High volume (long ride/run, doubles) | 8–10 g/kg/day | Big aerobic days |
| Very high / peak block | 10–12 g/kg/day | Heaviest weeks only |

**Protein:** ~1.6–2.2 g/kg/day, ~0.3 g/kg per meal, a dose within 30–60 min of hard sessions (crucial before a second session that day).

**In-session fueling (train your gut — this is a trainable skill):**

| Race duration / type | Carbs per hour | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sprint (< ~75 min) | Little/none; carb mouth rinse | Fitness > fuel here; if your race runs > 75 min, add ~30 g/hr |
| 1–2.5 hr (Olympic) | 30–60 g/hr | Single or mixed-source carbs |
| 70.3 (~4–6 hr) | 60–90 g/hr | Glucose:fructose ~1:0.8 to lift the ceiling |
| Ironman (~9–15 hr) | 90–120 g/hr | Bias intake to the bike; must be gut-trained |

**Fuel the bike, save the run:** the bike is where you can absorb fuel (aero-but-stable, lower GI stress); the run jostles the gut, so front-load carbs and fluid on the bike and take in less while running.

**Hydration & sodium:** ~500–800 ml/hr fluid, scaled to body size, heat, and sweat rate; **500–1000 mg sodium per litre** of fluid (heavy/salty sweaters 700–1000+; light sweaters lower). Sodium scales with sweat rate, not body weight. Aim to lose no more than ~2–3% body mass; **don't over-drink** — beyond thirst in long, hot races risks hyponatremia (<135 mmol/L).

**Golden rule:** nothing new on race day. Every gel, drink, bar, and bottle is rehearsed in training first.

## 7. Race Preparation by Distance

| Distance | Approx swim/bike/run | Dominant demand | Pacing & strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sprint** (~750m / 20km / 5km) | ~1–1.5 hr | Threshold/VO2, high intensity | Near-red the whole way; fast transitions decisive; minimal fueling |
| **Olympic** (1.5km / 40km / 10km) | ~2–3 hr | Threshold | Controlled-hard bike, strong run; 30–60 g carb/hr; smart pacing off the swim |
| **70.3** (1.9km / 90km / 21.1km) | ~4–6 hr | Sweet spot/tempo | Bike at ~75–85% FTP, run at controlled effort; 60–90 g carb/hr; discipline over ego |
| **Ironman** (3.8km / 180km / 42.2km) | ~9–15 hr | Aerobic/durability | Bike ~65–75% FTP (never surge), even/slightly negative run; 90–120 g carb/hr; the day is won by not blowing up |

**Universal race rules:** don't win the race in the swim, don't lose it on the bike — the bike sets up the run. Pace by power/HR/effort, not by the athletes around you. The longer the race, the more it's an eating-and-drinking contest with exercise attached.

## 8. Taper & Race Day

The taper sheds fatigue while keeping sharpness — not a shutdown. Cut volume **40–60% over the final 1–3 weeks (longer for IM), keep intensity via short race-pace "openers," keep session frequency.** Well-tapered triathletes improve ~2–3%.

| Lever | Do | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Volume | Reduce 40–60% (more for IM) | Sheds fatigue, restores force |
| Intensity | Keep short race-pace openers | Maintains feel and sharpness |
| Frequency | Keep most sessions, shorter | Preserves rhythm across all 3 sports |
| Duration | Sprint/Oly ~1 wk; 70.3 ~1.5–2 wk; IM ~2–3 wk | Longer races shed more fatigue |

**Race week:** dial in logistics, bike mechanical check, sleep bank, carb-load the final 1–2 days (long course: ~8–12 g/kg/day). **Race morning:** familiar carb-forward breakfast 3 hr out (~1–2 g/kg), top-up sip before start. **Execution:** warm up (easy swim + short bike/run openers), own your pacing plan, hit your fueling clock (eat/drink before you're hungry/thirsty), rehearse transitions in your head.

## 9. Staying Healthy — Load & Red Flags

The .01% difference is consecutive healthy, consistent weeks. Three sports means three injury profiles and shared systemic fatigue — respect both.

**Load rules:**
- Progress total weekly load ≤ ~10%/week; recovery week every 3–4 weeks.
- Add hard intensity **OR** big volume in a block — rarely both at once.
- Running carries the highest impact-injury risk — ramp run volume most conservatively.
- Sleep and fuel are training; under-eating (RED-S / low energy availability) tanks performance and health.

**STOP / assess signals:**
- Sharp or worsening joint pain (knee, Achilles, IT band, hip) — especially on the run; back off before it becomes a layoff.
- Shoulder pain in the pull-through — cut paddle/pull volume, ramp prehab.
- Persistent fatigue, elevated resting HR, poor sleep, stalling paces/power — under-recovery; take the down week.
- Any pain that alters your stroke, pedal, or gait — fix the cause; compensation spreads injury.

## 10. Four Triathlete Profiles

- **The First-Timer (Sprint, 4–6 hr/wk):** consistency over intensity; learn to swim in open water, ride safely, run off the bike; short weekly brick; finish strong and uninjured. Volume +10%/wk, recovery week every 4th.
- **The Age-Group Olympic Athlete (6–9 hr/wk):** balanced three-sport build, threshold focus, fast transitions, one quality brick/week; bias sessions to the limiter sport; ~1-wk taper.
- **The 70.3 Athlete (8–12 hr/wk):** sweet-spot bike volume, long run durability, race-simulation bricks, gut training to 60–90 g/hr; discipline pacing on the bike to protect the run; ~1.5–2-wk taper.
- **The Ironman Athlete (12–18+ hr/wk):** big aerobic base, weekly long ride + long run, brick specificity, carb-loading and 90–120 g/hr fueling rehearsed for months; durability and pacing over peak power; 2–3-wk taper.
