# PROJECT OSPREY — The CrossFit Blueprint
*Strength • engine • gymnastics — build broad, general, competition-ready fitness*

This is the CrossFit edition of the OSPREY Expert Coach program — the same engine, rebuilt for constantly varied, high-intensity functional fitness. It turns four onboarding answers into a periodized plan that develops strength, engine and gymnastics concurrently and peaks you for the Open. Constantly varied, but planned — not random.

## 1. How the Expert Coach Program Works

| # | Onboarding Input | What it drives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Experience & current capacity — training age, key benchmarks (Fran etc.), main lifts, sessions/week | Volume, and skill-vs-strength-vs-engine emphasis |
| 2 | Goal — general fitness, or compete (Open → Quarterfinals → Semifinals → Games) | Programming balance and the peak |
| 3 | Timeline — weeks to the Open or target event | Macrocycle: Base / Build / Competition / Deload |
| 4 | Constraints & injury history — schedule, equipment, shoulders / low back / knees | Volume, movement scaling, red flags |

**One-line philosophy:** Build strength, a big aerobic and anaerobic engine, and gymnastics skill concurrently — then periodize the emphasis so you peak for the Open without being fried. Mechanics, then consistency, then intensity.

## 2. Training Philosophy

CrossFit trains all ten general physical skills across three modalities — monostructural (cardio), gymnastics (bodyweight), weightlifting. The program is concurrent but periodized: emphasis shifts block to block so nothing regresses while you sharpen what the season demands. Intensity drives results, but only after mechanics and consistency are earned.

**Macrocycle**

| Phase | When | Emphasis | Looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base / Off-season | Post-Games, furthest out | Strength + aerobic base + skill acquisition | Higher strength volume, Z2 engine, learn skills unbroken |
| Build | Mid-season | Strength-endurance + threshold + skill under fatigue | Heavier metcons, interval engine, gymnastics volume |
| Competition / Open prep | Near | Mixed-modal peaking, test-specific | Open-style workouts, redline & pacing practice |
| Deload / Taper | Every 4–6 wks & Open weeks | Freshness | Lower volume, keep intensity sharp |

**Energy-system zones**

| System | Duration | Work : Rest | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Phosphagen / alactic | <10–15 s | 1:5–1:10 | Power, speed | Heavy singles, short sprints |
| Glycolytic / anaerobic | 30 s–2 min | 1:1–1:3 | Lactate tolerance | Fran, sprint intervals |
| Aerobic threshold | 3–10 min | Short rest | Sustainable power | Interval metcons |
| Aerobic base (Z2) | 20–60+ min | Continuous | Engine & recovery | Long row/run/bike easy |

## 3. The Key Sessions

- **Strength** — squat, press, deadlift and the Olympic lifts, by %1RM or RPE.
- **Olympic lifting** — technical work + power; snatch and clean & jerk.
- **Gymnastics skill** — pull-ups, chest-to-bar, muscle-ups, HSPU, handstand walk, toes-to-bar; skill first, then volume and skill-under-fatigue.
- **Metcons across time domains** — short (<5 min), medium (5–15), long (15+).
- **Engine** — Z2 aerobic base (row/bike/run) + intervals to raise threshold.
- **Benchmark testing** — the Girls and Heroes to measure progress and expose weaknesses.

## 4. Technique & Movement Standards

Charter: mechanics → consistency → intensity. Earn the position before speed/load. Train to competition movement standards (full lockout, chin over bar, hip crease below knee, ball to target). A rep with broken mechanics is a rep you'll pay for — scale load and movement to keep mechanics intact.

## 5. Strength & Supporting Work

| Phase | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Absolute strength + hypertrophy | Back squat, press, deadlift, accessory volume |
| Build | Strength-endurance + power | Touch-and-go barbell cycling, complexes, tempo |
| Competition | Maintenance + specificity | Heavy singles, mixed-modal peaking |

Accessory/midline work (posterior chain, upper back, shoulders, core) is the injury insurance that lets high-intensity volume stay safe.

## 6. Nutrition & Hydration

| Target | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Carbohydrate | 4–7 g/kg/day (up to 8) | Fuels high work capacity & two-a-days |
| Protein | 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day | Muscle repair across mixed training |
| Fat | Balance of calories | Round out energy needs |
| Intra-workout | Carbs on long sessions / doubles | Preserve second-session output |
| Hydration | To thirst + electrolytes | Sodium on long/hot sessions |

Fuel around the day: carb-forward pre-workout, protein + carbs within 30–60 min after (especially before a second session).

## 7. Competition Preparation

2026 season pathway: **Open** (Feb 26–Mar 16, three workouts over three weeks) → **Quarterfinals** (Mar 26–30, top 25% worldwide) → **Semifinals** (Apr–Jun, top ~2,000 per division, in-person or online) → **CrossFit Games** (July 24–26, San Jose; 30 men, 30 women, top 20 teams). Prep is mixed-modal peaking: rehearse the "unknown and unknowable," lock movement standards, drill pacing/transitions/redline management.

## 8. Taper & Competition Day

Deload every 4–6 weeks; manage fatigue across the three Open weeks (don't re-attempt without recovery). Taper into a competition by cutting volume while keeping intensity touches. Day: warm up fully and specifically, know movement standards and pacing plan, fuel light and familiar between heats.

## 9. Staying Healthy — Load & Red Flags

Shoulder pain from overhead/kipping volume; low-back pain when heavy metcons fatigue form (stop before mechanics break); novel high-volume eccentric work = real rhabdomyolysis risk (ramp gradually, hydrate; dark urine + severe soreness → seek care); any pain that changes a movement pattern → scale, fix the cause. Never trade form for a faster time.

## 10. Athlete Profiles

- **The Beginner** (general fitness, 3×/wk) — mechanics → consistency → intensity; scale everything, build base and skills.
- **The Intermediate Competitor** (Open-focused, 5–6×/wk) — concurrent strength/engine/gymnastics, peak for the Open.
- **The Masters Athlete** (40+) — recovery-biased volume, joint-friendly scaling, keep intensity smart.
- **The Elite / Semis-Games Hopeful** (two-a-days) — high periodized volume, surgical weakness work, sport-specific peaking, redline & pacing practice.
